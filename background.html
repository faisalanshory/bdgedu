Background

What is the reason behind EQUIPOTS?

Achieving inclusive and quality education for all reaffirms the belief that education is one of the most powerful and proven vehicles for sustainable development. This is also in accordance with Sustainable Development Goal number 4: "Quality Education". To support this goal, EQUIPOTS tries to approach the calculation of the quality of education through a spatial approach based on comparative information on the number of teachers and students

The concept of controlled classrooms emerged in the Roman Empire. The underlying logic is that limited class sizes will improve the quality of instruction. But on the other hand, low student and teacher ratios can indicate inequality in education or the recruitment of teachers who do not meet the needs and exceed the number of student registrations at all levels of education.

According to Sari Soegondo, a member of the Analytical and Capacity Development Partnership (ACDP), the ratio of student-teacher ratios is 20: 1, however, in Indonesia, the ratio is 15: 1 and is the lowest teacher ratio in the world. (taken fromhttps://www.beritasatu.com/)

Indonesia is very far away because it currently has a 15: 1 ratio due to lack of development in the quality of education so that in quantity there is an imbalance in the teacher-student ratio that looks very prominent in the regions.

On the other hand, low and evenly distributed student and teacher ratios can provide benefits including, greater individual attention, better class participation by students, better comprehension of topics taught, and improved academic performance.

From these problems, EQUIPOTS tried to provide a solution by utilizing data on the number of teachers, the number of students, and the pure participation rate (APM) in 9 years of education level. The aim is to find out whether education in the city of Bandung for each district level has good quality based on the information used.
